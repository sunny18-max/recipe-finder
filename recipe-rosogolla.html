Bengali

Rosogolla

2 hours Makes 20 pieces Vegetarian
4.9 (512 reviews)

Bengal's famous spongy, syrup-soaked cheese balls that are light, delicate and melt-in-your-mouth. These iconic white balls made from chhena (fresh cheese) and semolina are cooked in sugar syrup, absorbing just the right amount of sweetness while maintaining their pillowy texture.

Ingredients

For Chhena:

  • 2 liters full-fat milk
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • 1 tsp all-purpose flour

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 4 green cardamoms, crushed
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Make Chhena

Bring milk to boil. Add lemon juice gradually until milk curdles completely. Strain through muslin cloth and rinse under cold water.

Step 2: Prepare Cheese Dough

Hang curdled milk for 30 mins to drain. Then knead with semolina and flour for 15 mins until smooth and non-grainy.

Step 3: Shape Balls

Make small smooth balls (about 20g each) without cracks. Keep covered with damp cloth.

Step 4: Prepare Syrup

Boil sugar, water and cardamom to make thin syrup (1.5 string consistency).

Step 5: Cook Rosogollas

Gently drop balls into simmering syrup. Cover and cook 15 mins until doubled in size. Turn off heat and let cool in syrup.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Milk Tip: Full-fat cow milk works best for soft texture.

Kneading: Proper kneading prevents bursting during cooking.

Storage: Keep immersed in syrup for up to 5 days refrigerated.

Variation: Add saffron to syrup for kesar rosogolla.
