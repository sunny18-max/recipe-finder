Masala Dosa

45 mins Serves 3 Medium
4.5 (128 reviews)

Crispy golden crepes filled with spiced potato masala, served with coconut chutney and sambar. A South Indian breakfast classic that will transport your taste buds to the streets of Bangalore!

Ingredients

For Dosa Batter:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

For Potato Masala:

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

For Serving:

  • Coconut chutney
  • Sambar
  • Ghee or oil for cooking

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Batter

Soak rice and urad dal separately for 6 hours. Grind to a smooth batter, mix together with salt, and ferment overnight (8-12 hours). The batter should rise and become slightly frothy.

Step 2: Make Potato Masala

Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and curry leaves. When they splutter, add onions and green chilies, sauté until translucent. Add turmeric and mashed potatoes, mix well and cook for 5 minutes. Season with salt.

Step 3: Cook the Dosa

Heat a dosa tawa (griddle) on medium-high. Pour ladle of batter and spread in circular motion to make thin crepe. Drizzle ghee/oil around edges. Cook until golden and crisp.

Step 4: Assemble

When dosa turns golden, place 2-3 tablespoons of potato masala in center. Fold into triangle or roll. Serve immediately with coconut chutney and sambar.

Chef's Notes

Fermentation Tip: Keep batter in warm place (like oven with light on) for better fermentation. Ideal temperature is about 80°F (27°C).

Crispy Secret: Spread dosa thin and cook on medium-high heat. The tawa should be properly heated before pouring batter.

Variation: Try cheese masala dosa by adding grated cheese before folding. For extra crispiness, use ghee instead of oil.

Storage: Leftover batter can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. Bring to room temperature before using.
