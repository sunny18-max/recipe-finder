Dahi Vada

4 hours (includes soaking) Serves 6 Medium
4.7 (89 reviews)

Fluffy lentil dumplings soaked in creamy whipped yogurt and drizzled with tangy tamarind chutney and spicy mint chutney. This cooling North Indian chaat is perfect for hot summer days and festive occasions.

Ingredients

For Vada:

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Oil for deep frying

For Yogurt Mixture:

  • 3 cups thick yogurt (whipped)
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish:

  • Tamarind chutney
  • Mint-coriander chutney
  • Sev (fried gram flour noodles)
  • Chopped coriander leaves

Instructions

Step 1: Soak and Grind

Soak urad dal for 4 hours. Drain and grind to a smooth, fluffy batter with ginger and green chili. The batter should be thick enough to hold shape.

Step 2: Fry Vadas

Heat oil to 350Â°F. Wet your hands, take small portions of batter, shape into balls and gently slide into hot oil. Fry until golden brown (3-4 minutes per batch).

Step 3: Soak Vadas

Immediately transfer fried vadas to warm water for 15 minutes. Gently squeeze out water before assembling.

Step 4: Assemble

Whisk yogurt with sugar, salt and half the cumin powder. Arrange vadas on serving plate, pour yogurt over them. Drizzle with both chutneys, sprinkle remaining cumin powder, red chili powder, sev and coriander leaves.

Chef's Notes

Pro Tip: For extra fluffy vadas, grind the batter in intervals to prevent overheating and add 1 tbsp ice water while grinding.

Make Ahead: You can fry vadas 1 day in advance and store at room temperature. Soak in warm water before serving.

Serving Suggestion: Serve chilled for best texture contrast between cool yogurt and soft vadas.

Variation: Add boiled potato cubes between vada layers for Dahi Bhalla Chaat.
