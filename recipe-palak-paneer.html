North Indian

Palak Paneer

45 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian Healthy
4.9 (2,654 reviews)

The quintessential North Indian dish combining fresh spinach (palak) with soft paneer cubes in a creamy, aromatic gravy. This vibrant green curry is packed with nutrients from the spinach, balanced by the richness of paneer and cream, and elevated with a blend of spices that enhance but don't overpower the natural flavors.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh spinach (palak)
  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped

Spices:

  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 tsp kasuri methi
  • Salt to taste

For Tempering:

  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 dried red chili

Instructions

Step 1: Blanch Spinach

Boil water, blanch spinach for 2 mins. Drain, rinse in cold water. Blend to smooth paste.

Step 2: Prepare Base

Heat ghee, temper cumin. Add onions, sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, sauté.

Step 3: Cook Tomatoes

Add tomatoes, cook until soft. Add all dry spices except garam masala, cook 2 mins.

Step 4: Combine Spinach

Add spinach puree, simmer 5 mins. Add paneer cubes, garam masala, kasuri methi.

Step 5: Prepare Tadka

Heat ghee, add cumin, garlic and red chili. Pour over curry immediately before serving.

Step 6: Serve Hot

Garnish with cream if desired. Serve with naan or jeera rice.

Chef's Notes

Time Saver: Use store-bought tandoori chicken for quicker version.

Vegetarian Option: Substitute chicken with paneer or mushrooms.

Key Ingredient: Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek) is essential for authentic flavor.

Make Ahead: Tastes even better next day as flavors develop.
