Rasgulla

1 hour 30 mins Serves 6 Medium
4.7 (356 reviews)

Soft, spongy Bengali rasgullas soaked in light sugar syrup. These melt-in-mouth cheese balls are surprisingly easy to make at home with just three ingredients - milk, lemon juice and sugar.

Ingredients

For Chenna (Paneer):

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 cup ice water

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Make Chenna

Boil milk. When it boils, turn off heat and add lemon juice. Stir until milk curdles completely. Drain in muslin cloth and rinse with ice water.

Step 2: Knead Chenna

Hang chenna for 30 minutes to drain. Then knead for 10-15 minutes until smooth and grease-free.

Step 3: Make Balls

Divide dough into 15-18 equal portions. Roll into smooth balls without cracks.

Step 4: Prepare Syrup

Boil sugar, water and cardamom to make thin syrup (1.5 thread consistency).

Step 5: Cook Rasgullas

Gently drop balls in boiling syrup. Cover and cook for 15 minutes. They will double in size. Cool completely before serving.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Milk Tip: Use full-fat milk for soft rasgullas. Low-fat milk makes them hard.

Kneading Secret: Proper kneading is crucial for spongy texture - about 10-15 minutes.

Storage: Keep immersed in syrup for up to 3 days refrigerated.

Variation: Add saffron to syrup for kesar rasgulla.
