Gujarati Undhiyu

2 hours Serves 6 Vegetarian Seasonal
4.6 (1,542 reviews)

The iconic Gujarati winter vegetable medley cooked with a distinctive blend of spices and muthiyas (fried dumplings). This elaborate dish gets its name from the Gujarati word "undhu" meaning upside down, traditionally cooked in earthen pots buried underground with fire on top. Packed with seasonal vegetables like purple yam, sweet potatoes, and fenugreek dumplings in a sweet-spicy coconut-based gravy.

Ingredients

Main Vegetables:

  • 1 cup surti papdi (flat beans)
  • 1 small eggplant, quartered
  • 1 cup small potatoes
  • 1 cup purple yam (kand), cubed
  • 1 cup sweet potato, cubed
  • 1/2 cup fresh tuvar dana (pigeon peas)

For Muthiya (Dumplings):

  • 1 cup methi (fenugreek) leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1/4 cup wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp ginger-chili paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Oil for frying

For Masala:

  • 1/2 cup fresh coconut, grated
  • 2 tbsp coriander-cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp jaggery
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup cilantro, chopped

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Muthiya

Mix all muthiya ingredients, shape into cylinders, deep fry until golden. Set aside.

Step 2: Prep Vegetables

Clean and cut all vegetables. Soak papdi and tuvar dana in water for 30 mins.

Step 3: Make Masala Paste

Grind coconut, coriander-cumin powder, ginger-garlic paste with little water to smooth paste.

Step 4: Layer Vegetables

In heavy-bottomed pan, layer vegetables. Add masala paste, jaggery, salt between layers.

Step 5: Cook Undhiyu

Add water (1/2 cup), cover and cook on low heat for 45 mins. Add muthiya last 15 mins.

Step 6: Finish & Serve

Garnish with cilantro and garam masala. Serve with puri and shrikhand.

Chef's Notes

Traditional Method: Authentic undhiyu is cooked in earthen pots buried upside down in coal.

Seasonal Tip: Best made in winter when fresh fenugreek and fresh beans are available.

Shortcut: Use frozen mixed vegetables if fresh ones aren't available.

Serving Suggestion: Tastes even better the next day as flavors develop.
