Soft Roti/Chapati

45 mins Makes 8 Vegetarian Whole Wheat
4.7 (5,421 reviews)

The everyday Indian flatbread made with whole wheat flour, water and salt. These soft, pliable rotis puff up beautifully when cooked correctly, with a slight char from direct flame cooking. A staple across Indian households, perfect for scooping up curries and vegetables, or enjoyed simply with a dab of ghee.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 3/4 cup warm water (approx)
  • Extra flour for dusting
  • Ghee for brushing (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Dough

Mix flour and salt. Add oil, mix. Gradually add water to form soft dough. Knead 8-10 mins.

Step 2: Rest Dough

Cover with damp cloth, rest for 30 mins. This develops gluten for soft rotis.

Step 3: Divide & Shape

Divide into 8 balls. Roll each into smooth ball between palms.

Step 4: Roll Rotis

Dust with flour, roll into 6-7" circles, rotating frequently for even thickness.

Step 5: Cook Rotis

Heat tawa, cook roti 30 secs. Flip, cook other side. Press with cloth to puff up.

Step 6: Finish & Serve

Optionally brush with ghee. Serve immediately with curry or vegetables.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Dough Tip: The dough should be stiffer than roti dough for crisp samosas.

Frying Secret: Maintain medium heat - too hot will brown outside before inside cooks.

Variation: Try minced meat or paneer fillings for non-veg versions.

Storage: Freeze uncooked samosas for up to a month.
