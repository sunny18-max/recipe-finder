Tantanmen

1 hour Serves 4 Medium
4.6 (142 reviews)

A Japanese adaptation of Sichuan dan dan noodles featuring a rich, spicy sesame-peanut broth with ground pork topping. This comforting bowl combines nutty, spicy, and savory flavors in perfect harmony.

Ingredients

For Broth:

  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp chili oil

For Meat Topping:

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 2 tbsp doubanjiang (spicy bean paste)
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp mirin

For Assembly:

  • 4 servings ramen noodles
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 tbsp toasted sesame seeds

Instructions

Step 1: Make Broth

Whisk together hot chicken broth, tahini, peanut butter, soy sauce and chili oil until smooth. Keep warm.

Step 2: Cook Pork

Brown ground pork in skillet. Add doubanjiang, sake and mirin. Cook until liquid evaporates.

Step 3: Cook Noodles

Boil noodles according to package, then drain.

Step 4: Assemble

Divide broth among bowls. Add noodles, then pork. Top with egg, green onions and sesame seeds.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Shortcut: Use pre-made tonkotsu broth base if short on time.

Traditional Tip: The broth should be cloudy white from the emulsified fat and marrow.

Make Ahead: Broth can be made 3 days in advance and reheated.

Vegetarian Option: Substitute with mushroom broth and tofu.
