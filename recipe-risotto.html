Mushroom Risotto

40 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian
4.8 (376 reviews)

Creamy Italian rice dish cooked slowly with arborio rice, white wine, and mushroom stock, loaded with sautéed wild mushrooms and finished with parmesan and butter. This comforting risotto achieves perfect al dente texture with a luxurious, velvety consistency that coats each grain of rice.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 300g arborio rice
  • 400g mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, oyster)
  • 1.5 liters mushroom or vegetable stock
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced

For Cooking:

  • 150ml dry white wine
  • 50g butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For Finishing:

  • 50g parmesan, grated
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Mushrooms

Clean and slice mushrooms. Sauté half in butter until golden. Set aside for garnish.

Step 2: Sauté Aromatics

In same pan, heat oil and butter. Cook onions until soft, add garlic and remaining mushrooms.

Step 3: Toast Rice

Add rice, stir to coat in fat. Cook 2 minutes until translucent at edges.

Step 4: Deglaze

Pour in wine, stir until absorbed. Begin adding hot stock, one ladle at a time.

Step 5: Finish

When rice is al dente (about 18 mins), stir in parmesan and reserved mushrooms. Season and garnish with parsley.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Stock Tip: Keep stock simmering throughout cooking.

Rice Note: Stir constantly but not vigorously to release starch.

Variation: Add truffle oil for luxury version.

Leftovers: Risotto cakes make excellent next-day meal.
