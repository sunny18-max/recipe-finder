Bengali

Mishti Doi

8 hours (includes setting) Serves 6 Vegetarian
4.9 (412 reviews)

Bengal's famous sweetened yogurt dessert with caramelized notes, traditionally set in earthen pots. This creamy, slightly tangy delicacy has a distinctive caramel flavor from slow-cooked reduced milk and jaggery, with a texture that's simultaneously dense and velvety smooth.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • Â½ cup jaggery (or brown sugar)
  • 2 tbsp yogurt (as starter)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)

For Flavoring:

  • 1 green cardamom, powdered
  • Pinch of saffron strands (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Reduce Milk

Boil milk in heavy-bottomed pan, stirring frequently until reduced to half (about 45 mins). Scrape sides to incorporate cream.

Step 2: Add Sweetener

When milk is reduced, add jaggery and stir until completely dissolved. Cook 2-3 minutes more. Let cool to lukewarm.

Step 3: Add Starter

Whisk yogurt with 2 tbsp of the warm milk mixture, then stir back into main pot. Add cardamom and saffron if using.

Step 4: Set

Pour into earthen pots or glass bowls. Cover and keep in warm place 6-8 hours until set.

Step 5: Chill

Refrigerate at least 4 hours before serving. The doi will thicken further when chilled.

Chef's Notes

Traditional Method: Earthen pots impart distinctive earthy flavor.

Sweetness: Adjust jaggery to taste - Bengali version is quite sweet.

Texture Tip: Don't disturb while setting for smooth texture.

Serving: Garnish with chopped nuts or edible rose petals.
