Punjabi

Sarson Ka Saag

4 hours Serves 6 Vegetarian
4.9 (1,542 reviews)

The quintessential winter dish from Punjab - mustard greens slow-cooked with spices and finished with a glorious tempering of garlic and ghee. This earthy, nutritious greens preparation is traditionally served with makki ki roti (corn flatbread) and a dollop of white butter.

Ingredients

Main Greens:

  • 1 kg mustard leaves (sarson)
  • 500g spinach (palak)
  • 250g bathua (chenopodium) - optional

For Tempering:

  • Â½ cup ghee
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp cornmeal (makki ka atta)
  • Salt to taste

For Final Tadka:

  • 4 tbsp ghee
  • 10 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 2 dry red chilies

Instructions

Step 1: Clean Greens

Wash all greens thoroughly. Roughly chop mustard leaves and spinach.

Step 2: Pressure Cook

Cook greens with 1 cup water in pressure cooker for 6-7 whistles until completely soft.

Step 3: Mash

Mash cooked greens with wooden masher or hand blender. Mix in cornmeal.

Step 4: Prepare Base

Heat ghee, temper cumin. Add onions, ginger-garlic paste, then tomatoes. Cook until mushy.

Step 5: Combine

Add mashed greens to onion-tomato mixture. Simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 6: Final Tadka

Heat ghee, fry garlic until golden. Add red chilies and pour over saag.

Chef's Notes

Greens Ratio: Mustard leaves should dominate (60%) for authentic flavor.

Texture Tip: Coarse mashing gives traditional texture - don't over-blend.

Cornmeal Trick: Helps thicken and gives characteristic yellow hue.

Serving Tradition: Always serve with white butter (makhan) on top.
