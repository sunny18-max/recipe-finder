Pav Bhaji

45 mins Serves 4 Medium Spicy
4.9 (512 reviews)

A Mumbai street food legend - spicy mashed vegetable curry served with butter-laden pav (bread rolls). Born as a quick meal for textile mill workers, this flavor bomb is now India's favorite comfort food.

Ingredients

For Bhaji:

  • 3 potatoes, boiled
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 capsicum, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 lemon, wedged

For Serving:

  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions

Step 1: Cook Vegetables

Pressure cook potatoes, cauliflower, peas with 1 cup water for 2 whistles. Mash coarsely.

Step 2: Prepare Base

Heat 2 tbsp butter in large pan. Saut√© onions until translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste, cook for 1 minute.

Step 3: Make Bhaji

Add tomato puree, cook until oil separates. Add all spices, mashed vegetables and 1 cup water. Simmer for 10 minutes.

Step 4: Finish

Add capsicum, remaining butter and cilantro. Mash to desired consistency.

Step 5: Serve

Toast pav in butter. Serve bhaji hot with chopped onions, lemon wedges and extra butter.

Chef's Notes

Masala Tip: Everest or Badshah pav bhaji masala work best. Adjust quantity to taste.

Texture: Bhaji should be thick but spreadable - add water as needed while cooking.

Restaurant Style: Use extra butter and serve on cast iron tawa for authentic experience.

Variations: Add grated beetroot for color or paneer for protein boost.
