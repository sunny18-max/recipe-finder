Misal Pav

1 hour Serves 4 Spicy
4.8 (289 reviews)

Pune's signature spicy sprout curry topped with farsan, onions, and cilantro, served with soft pav. This protein-packed breakfast dish comes with a spicy kick and is often customized with varying heat levels at local eateries.

Ingredients

For Sprouts:

  • 1 cup moth beans (matki), sprouted
  • 1/2 cup moong sprouts
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • Salt to taste

For Masala:

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tbsp goda masala
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp jaggery
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida
  • 8-10 curry leaves

For Serving:

  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • 1 cup farsan (mixed savory snacks)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 lemon, wedged

Instructions

Step 1: Cook Sprouts

Pressure cook sprouts with turmeric and salt for 3 whistles. Drain excess water.

Step 2: Prepare Masala

Heat oil, sauté onions until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, cook for 1 minute. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.

Step 3: Make Curry

Add all spices, jaggery and coconut. Mix well, add cooked sprouts and 2 cups water. Simmer for 15 minutes.

Step 4: Temper

Heat ghee in small pan, add tempering ingredients. Pour over curry, garnish with cilantro.

Step 5: Assemble

In serving bowl: sprouts curry → farsan → onions → cilantro. Serve with pav and lemon wedges.

Chef's Notes

Goda Masala: Essential for authentic flavor - available at Maharashtrian grocery stores.

Spice Level: Adjust chili powder quantity. Traditional Puneri misal is very spicy.

Variations: Kolhapuri misal uses different spice blend, Nagpuri version is less spicy.

Toppings: Add diced potatoes or poha for different textures.
