Pizza Margherita

2 hours Serves 4 Easy
4.9 (256 reviews)

The classic Neapolitan pizza featuring a thin, chewy crust topped with simple, fresh ingredients: tomato sauce, mozzarella, and basil. A perfect representation of the Italian flag's colors!

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 500g tipo 00 flour
  • 325ml warm water
  • 7g active dry yeast
  • 10g salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the Toppings:

  • 200ml San Marzano tomato sauce
  • 200g fresh mozzarella di bufala
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Make the Dough

Dissolve yeast in warm water. Mix flour and salt, then add yeast mixture and olive oil. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth. Let rise for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.

Step 2: Prepare Toppings

Drain and slice mozzarella. Crush San Marzano tomatoes by hand for sauce, season with salt. Tear basil leaves just before using.

Step 3: Shape the Pizza

Divide dough into 4 balls. On a floured surface, stretch each ball into a thin 12-inch circle using your hands (no rolling pin).

Step 4: Bake

Preheat oven to maximum temperature (500°F/260°C) with pizza stone. Spread tomato sauce, add mozzarella. Bake for 8-10 minutes until crust is golden. Add fresh basil and drizzle with olive oil.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Dough Tip: For best results, let dough cold ferment in fridge for 24-48 hours for deeper flavor.

Oven Hack: If you don't have a pizza stone, use an upside-down baking sheet preheated in the oven.

Variation: Try adding a few slices of fresh tomato under the mozzarella for extra freshness.

Storage: Dough can be frozen for up to 3 months. Thaw in refrigerator before using.
