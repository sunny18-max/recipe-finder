The Essence of East Indian Flavors

East Indian cuisine is characterized by its subtle yet complex flavors, with an emphasis on fish, rice, and mustard oil. The region's riverine geography and tropical climate have shaped a culinary tradition that's both light and deeply flavorful.

From the sweet delights of Bengal to the fiery curries of Assam and the temple cuisine of Odisha, East Indian food offers a diverse palette of tastes that reflects the region's rich cultural heritage.

Regional Specialties

Discover the diverse culinary traditions across East India

West Bengal

Land of fish curries, sweets like rasgulla and sandesh, and mustard-based flavors.

Odisha

Known for its temple cuisine, pakhala bhata, and chhena-based sweets.

Assam

Famous for bamboo shoot dishes, fish tenga, and khar - an alkaline preparation.

Bihar & Jharkhand

Home to litti chokha, sattu preparations, and thekua sweets.
