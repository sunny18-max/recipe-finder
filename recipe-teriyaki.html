Teriyaki Chicken

30 mins Serves 4 Easy
4.5 (203 reviews)

Juicy chicken glazed with a glossy, sweet-savory teriyaki sauce made from scratch. This authentic Japanese version is less sweet than American adaptations, with perfect caramelization and tender meat every time.

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp sake (optional)
  • 1 tsp grated ginger

For the Teriyaki Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (optional)

For Serving:

  • Steamed rice
  • Steamed vegetables
  • Sesame seeds
  • Green onions, sliced

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Chicken

Pat chicken dry and prick skin side with fork to help render fat. Mix with sake and ginger, let marinate 10 minutes.

Step 2: Make Sauce

Combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar in small saucepan. Simmer 3 minutes until slightly thickened. For thicker sauce, mix cornstarch with 1 tbsp water and stir in.

Step 3: Cook Chicken

Heat oil in skillet over medium. Cook chicken skin-side down 5 minutes until golden. Flip and cook 4 minutes. Pour off excess fat.

Step 4: Glaze

Add sauce to pan, spooning over chicken as it reduces. Cook 2-3 minutes until glossy and caramelized. Rest 5 minutes before slicing.

Chef's Notes

Cut Tip: Thighs stay juicier than breasts, but you can substitute if preferred.

Authentic Touch: Use Japanese soy sauce (shoyu) and real mirin (not "aji-mirin").

Variation: Try with salmon, beef, or tofu for different protein options.

Storage: Sauce keeps refrigerated for 2 weeks. Cooked chicken keeps 3 days.
