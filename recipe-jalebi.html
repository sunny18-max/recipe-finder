Jalebi

1 hour Serves 4 Medium
4.8 (412 reviews)

Crispy, syrupy swirls of fermented batter, jalebi is India's favorite sweet snack. These bright orange coils are surprisingly easy to make at home with our foolproof recipe.

Ingredients

For Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric (for color)

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Few saffron strands

For Frying:

  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Instructions

Step 1: Make Batter

Mix all batter ingredients to make smooth flowing consistency. Cover and ferment overnight or 8-10 hours.

Step 2: Prepare Syrup

Boil sugar and water to 1-string consistency. Add cardamom, lemon juice and saffron. Keep warm.

Step 3: Fry Jalebis

Heat oil. Pour batter in squeeze bottle or piping bag. Make spiral shapes in hot oil. Fry until crisp.

Step 4: Soak in Syrup

Dip hot jalebis in warm syrup for 30 seconds. Drain excess syrup and serve warm.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Fermentation Tip: In cold climates, keep batter in warm place or oven with light on.

Consistency: Batter should be like dosa batter - not too thick or thin.

Serving Suggestion: Best enjoyed warm with rabri or milk.

Quick Version: Use 1/4 tsp yeast instead of overnight fermentation.
