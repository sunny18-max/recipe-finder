Bengali

Pantua

1.5 hours Makes 15 Medium
4.4 (156 reviews)

Bengal's version of gulab jamun, these deep-fried chhena balls are caramelized to perfection before soaking in fragrant sugar syrup. Distinguished by their reddish-brown crust and soft, juicy interior.

Ingredients

For Pantua:

  • 2 cups chhena (fresh paneer/cottage cheese)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp khoya (optional)
  • Ghee/oil for deep frying

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 3 cups water
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 1 tsp rose water
  • 1/2 tsp saffron strands (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Chhena

Knead fresh chhena for 10 minutes until smooth. Add semolina, flour, baking soda and khoya (if using). Knead again to form soft dough.

Step 2: Shape Pantua

Divide dough into 15 portions. Roll into smooth balls, then slightly elongate to oval shapes (about 1.5 inch long).

Step 3: Make Sugar Syrup

Boil sugar, water and cardamom to 1-string consistency (about 10 minutes). Add rose water and saffron. Keep warm.

Step 4: Fry Pantua

Heat ghee/oil on medium. Fry pantua in batches until deep reddish-brown (about 5-6 minutes). Drain on paper towels.

Step 5: Soak in Syrup

Immediately transfer fried pantua to warm syrup. Soak for at least 2 hours before serving.

Chef's Notes

Chhena Texture: Should be moist but not watery. Excess moisture will cause pantua to break while frying.

Frying Tip: Maintain medium heat for even browning - high heat will burn outside before inside cooks.

Traditional Touch: Some recipes call for dipping in sugar syrup before frying for extra caramelization.

Serving: Best served warm with syrup. Can be stored refrigerated for up to 5 days.
