Kerala

Karimeen Pollichathu

1 hour Serves 2 Medium Spicy
4.8 (156 reviews)

Kerala's iconic pearl spot fish marinated in spicy masala, wrapped in banana leaf and pan-fried to perfection. The banana leaf imparts an earthy aroma while keeping the fish moist and flavorful - a signature dish from Kerala's backwaters.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 2 whole pearl spot fish (karimeen)
  • 2 banana leaves (30x30cm)
  • 3 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 lime
  • Salt to taste

For Masala:

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 4-5 dried red chilies
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger, chopped
  • 1 tbsp garlic, chopped
  • 2 sprigs curry leaves
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp kokum (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Fish

Clean fish, make 3-4 diagonal cuts on each side. Marinate with lime juice and salt for 15 minutes.

Step 2: Make Masala

Dry roast coriander, fennel, red chilies until fragrant. Grind with coconut, turmeric, ginger, garlic to coarse paste.

Step 3: Assemble

Heat 1 tbsp oil, saut√© onions and curry leaves. Mix with ground masala. Stuff fish cavities and coat exterior.

Step 4: Wrap

Briefly heat banana leaves over flame to soften. Place fish in center, drizzle oil, wrap tightly like parcel.

Step 5: Cook

Heat tawa, cook parcels 8-10 minutes each side on medium-low heat. Serve in leaf wrapping.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Fish Alternative: If pearl spot unavailable, use pomfret or sea bass.

Banana Leaf Tip: Wipe leaves with damp cloth but don't wash - preserves natural wax coating.

Traditional Method: Originally cooked between two heated clay tiles (kallu).

Serving: Unwrap at table to release aromatic steam - serve with kappa (tapioca) or rice.
