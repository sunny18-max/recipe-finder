Authentic Pad Thai

30 mins Serves 2 Medium
4.3 (97 reviews)

Stir-fried rice noodles with eggs, tofu, shrimp, and a perfect balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. Topped with crushed peanuts and fresh lime, this iconic Thai street food dish is a symphony of textures and tastes!

Ingredients

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 3 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tsp soy sauce

For the Noodles:

  • 8 oz rice noodles (flat, 1/4-inch wide)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup firm tofu, cubed
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup garlic chives or green onions
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Noodles

Soak rice noodles in warm water for 20-30 minutes until pliable but still firm. Drain and set aside.

Step 2: Make Sauce

Mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, vinegar, and soy sauce in a bowl until sugar dissolves. Adjust to taste - it should be sweet, sour, and salty.

Step 3: Stir Fry

Heat oil in wok over high heat. Add garlic, then tofu and shrimp. Push to one side, crack eggs into wok and scramble. Add noodles and sauce, toss everything together.

Step 4: Finish & Serve

Add bean sprouts and chives, toss briefly. Serve immediately topped with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and extra bean sprouts on the side.

Chef's Notes

Noodle Tip: Don't oversoak noodles - they'll continue cooking in the wok. They should be bendable but still have a bite.

Protein Options: Substitute chicken for shrimp or make vegetarian with extra tofu and mushrooms.

Authentic Touch: Look for preserved radish (optional) in Asian markets for traditional flavor.

Leftovers: Best eaten fresh but can be refrigerated for 1 day. Reheat in a wok with a splash of water.
