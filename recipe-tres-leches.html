Mexican

Tres Leches Cake

1 hour 4 hrs soaking Serves 12
4.7 (389 reviews)

Ultra-light sponge cake soaked in a mixture of three milks, topped with whipped cream and fresh berries. This iconic Mexican dessert is wonderfully moist without being soggy, with the perfect balance of sweetness and richness that makes it irresistible.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 5 large eggs, separated
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the Three Milks:

  • 1 (14 oz) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 (12 oz) can evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the Topping:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 3 tbsp powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon for dusting
  • Fresh berries for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Make Cake

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Sift flour with baking powder and salt. Beat egg whites to soft peaks, gradually add sugar until stiff peaks form. Beat in yolks one at a time. Fold in flour mixture alternately with milk. Pour into greased 9x13 pan and bake 25-30 minutes until golden.

Step 2: Prepare Milk Mixture

Whisk together condensed milk, evaporated milk, heavy cream and vanilla in bowl. When cake is done, cool 10 minutes, then pierce all over with fork. Slowly pour milk mixture over cake, allowing it to absorb.

Step 3: Soak Cake

Refrigerate cake at least 4 hours (preferably overnight) to fully absorb milks.

Step 4: Add Topping

Whip cream with powdered sugar and vanilla to stiff peaks. Spread over chilled cake. Dust with cinnamon and garnish with berries.

Step 5: Serve Chilled

Cut into squares and serve cold. The cake keeps well refrigerated for 3 days.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Sponge Cake Tip: Don't overmix after adding flour to maintain airy texture.

Milk Absorption: Pour milk mixture slowly in stages for even soaking.

Flavor Variations: Add 1 tbsp rum or 1 tsp cinnamon to milk mixture.

Presentation: For individual servings, bake in muffin tins and top with whipped cream rosettes.
