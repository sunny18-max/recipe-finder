Punjabi Special

Dahi Bhalla

5 hours (includes soaking) Serves 8 Medium
4.9 (124 reviews)

The Punjabi version of dahi vada with larger, softer bhallas soaked in sweetened yogurt and loaded with chutneys, spices, and crunchy toppings. A must-have at weddings and festive gatherings in North India.

Ingredients

For Bhalla:

  • 1 cup urad dal
  • 2 tbsp moong dal (optional for softness)
  • 1 tsp crushed black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Oil for deep frying

For Yogurt Mix:

  • 4 cups hung curd (thick yogurt)
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tbsp chaat masala
  • 1/2 cup milk (to adjust consistency)

For Layering:

  • 1 cup boiled potato cubes
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/2 cup sweet tamarind chutney
  • 1/4 cup green chutney
  • 1/2 cup nylon sev

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Batter

Soak dals together for 6 hours. Grind to smooth batter with minimal water. Add baking soda and black pepper, whisk until fluffy.

Step 2: Fry Bhallas

Heat oil to medium. Drop lemon-sized batter portions into oil. Fry on medium-low until golden brown (5-6 minutes each).

Step 3: Soak Bhallas

Soak fried bhallas in lukewarm water for 30 minutes. Gently press between palms to remove excess water.

Step 4: Assemble

Layer in dish: bhallas → potatoes → chickpeas → whipped sweet yogurt → chutneys → sev. Repeat layers. Chill for 1 hour before serving.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Texture Secret: Adding moong dal makes bhallas extra soft and porous to absorb more yogurt.

Festive Version: For weddings, add pomegranate seeds and silver leaf (varak) garnish.

Yogurt Tip: Hung curd works best - tie regular yogurt in muslin for 2 hours to drain whey.

Serving Style: In Punjab, often served in individual clay pots (kullads) for earthy flavor.
