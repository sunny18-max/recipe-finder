Risotto ai Funghi

45 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian
4.9 (421 reviews)

A luxurious mushroom risotto featuring a mix of wild and cultivated mushrooms, with their deep umami flavors infusing every grain of rice. This earthy, comforting dish gets its rich flavor from porcini mushrooms and a finishing touch of truffle oil for extra decadence.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 320g carnaroli or arborio rice
  • 1.5 liters mushroom or vegetable stock
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 120ml dry white wine

Mushrooms:

  • 30g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 300g mixed fresh mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, oyster)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves

For Finishing:

  • 60g butter
  • 60g parmesan, grated
  • 2 tbsp truffle oil (optional)
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Mushrooms

Soak porcini in 1 cup hot water for 20 mins. Strain, reserving liquid. Slice fresh mushrooms.

Step 2: Cook Mushrooms

Sauté fresh mushrooms in butter until golden. Add garlic, thyme, and soaked porcini. Cook 2 mins.

Step 3: Sauté Onion

In separate pan, cook onion in butter until soft. Add rice and toast 2 minutes.

Step 4: Deglaze

Pour in wine and stir until absorbed. Begin adding hot stock and porcini soaking liquid (strained).

Step 5: Finish

When rice is almost done (15 mins), stir in cooked mushrooms. Off heat, stir in remaining butter, parmesan and truffle oil. Garnish with parsley.

Chef's Notes

Mushroom Tip: Use at least 3 varieties for complex flavor.

Vegetarian Option: Use vegetable stock and vegetarian parmesan.

Luxury Version: Add fresh black truffle shavings before serving.

Stock Note: Mushroom stock enhances the umami flavor.
