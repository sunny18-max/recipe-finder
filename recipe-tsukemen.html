Japanese

Tsukemen

4 hours Serves 4 Medium
4.6 (298 reviews)

Japanese dipping ramen featuring thick, chewy noodles served separately from an intensely flavored concentrated broth. This summer favorite allows you to control the flavor intensity as you dip each bite of noodles into the rich, umami-packed sauce.

Ingredients

For Dipping Broth:

  • 10 cups pork or chicken broth
  • 1 cup dashi stock
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup bonito flakes
  • 1 piece kombu (4x4 inch)

For Noodles:

  • 1kg fresh extra-thick ramen noodles
  • 1 tbsp baking soda (for alkaline water)

For Toppings:

  • 8 slices chashu pork
  • 4 marinated soft-boiled eggs
  • 1 cup menma (bamboo shoots)
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 4 sheets nori
  • 1/2 cup chopped pork fat (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Reduce Broth

Combine all broth ingredients except bonito flakes. Simmer 3 hours until reduced by half. Add bonito flakes last 10 minutes, then strain.

Step 2: Prepare Noodles

Bring large pot of water with baking soda to boil. Cook noodles 1 minute longer than package instructions for extra chewiness.

Step 3: Chill Noodles

Rinse cooked noodles under cold water to stop cooking. Drain well and arrange on serving plates.

Step 4: Final Broth Prep

Skim fat from broth and reserve for topping. Heat broth until piping hot before serving.

Step 5: Serve

Divide broth among small bowls. Arrange toppings on noodle plates. Dip noodles into broth to enjoy.

Chef's Notes

Noodle Tip: Use specially made tsukemen noodles for authentic texture.

Broth Intensity: The dipping broth should be 2-3x more concentrated than regular ramen.

Summer Serving: Traditionally eaten cold in summer but can be served hot.

Customization: Add chili oil or citrus to adjust flavor to taste.
