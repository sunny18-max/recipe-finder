Odia Dalma

45 mins Serves 6 Vegetarian High Protein
4.9 (3,217 reviews)

The quintessential comfort food of Odisha, Dalma combines lentils with seasonal vegetables in a lightly spiced gravy. This temple-style preparation is offered as bhog to Lord Jagannath and forms the nutritional backbone of Odia cuisine with its perfect balance of proteins and vegetables.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon pea lentils)
  • 1/2 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (pumpkin, raw banana, eggplant, papaya)
  • 1 raw mango (diced) - seasonal
  • 1 tbsp grated coconut
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 dry red chili
  • 1 tsp panch phoron

Spice Paste:

  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions

Step 1: Cook Lentils

Wash and soak dals for 30 minutes. Pressure cook with turmeric and 3 cups water for 3 whistles until completely soft.

Step 2: Prepare Vegetables

Chop all vegetables into large chunks. Hard vegetables like pumpkin should be smaller than softer ones like eggplant.

Step 3: Make Spice Paste

Grind green chilies, cumin seeds and ginger-garlic with water to make a smooth paste.

Step 4: Combine & Cook

In a heavy pot, add cooked dal, vegetables, raw mango and spice paste. Simmer for 15 minutes until vegetables are tender.

Step 5: Tempering

Heat ghee, add all tempering ingredients. When aromatic, pour over cooked dalma. Garnish with grated coconut.

Chef's Notes

Temple Style: Authentic Puri temple dalma never uses onion or garlic.

Seasonal Variations: Winter dalma includes more root vegetables, summer version uses more gourds.

Texture Tip: The dal should be slightly thicker than regular dal, almost stew-like.

Serving Tradition: Always served with ghee-drizzled rice as the first course in Odia meals.
