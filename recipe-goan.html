Authentic Goan Fish Curry

50 mins Serves 4 Non-Vegetarian Medium Spice
4.7 (1,892 reviews)

The iconic coconut-based fish curry from Goa, featuring fresh seafood simmered in a fiery red gravy with tamarind and coconut milk. This Catholic-style curry gets its vibrant color from Kashmiri chilies and its distinctive tang from vinegar, creating a perfect balance of spicy, sour and creamy flavors that's best enjoyed with steamed rice or poi (Goan bread).

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 500g kingfish or pomfret (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp coconut vinegar (or toddy vinegar)
  • 1 small ball of tamarind
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil

For Masala Paste:

  • 8-10 Kashmiri red chilies
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 8 garlic cloves
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut

For Tempering:

  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 10 curry leaves
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Masala

Soak red chilies, coriander and cumin in warm water for 20 mins. Grind with turmeric, garlic, ginger and coconut to smooth paste.

Step 2: Extract Tamarind

Soak tamarind in 1/2 cup warm water for 15 mins. Squeeze and strain to get pulp.

Step 3: Cook Base

Heat oil, temper mustard seeds. Add curry leaves and onions. Saut√© until golden. Add masala paste and cook for 5 mins.

Step 4: Prepare Gravy

Add tamarind water, vinegar and 1 cup water. Bring to boil and simmer for 10 mins.

Step 5: Cook Fish

Gently add fish pieces. Cook covered on low heat for 8-10 mins until fish is done.

Step 6: Finish

Add coconut milk and heat through without boiling. Adjust consistency with water if needed.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Fish Selection: Firm fish like kingfish, pomfret or mackerel work best.

Vinegar Tip: Coconut vinegar gives authentic flavor but white vinegar can substitute.

Heat Control: Kashmiri chilies provide color without excessive heat.

Serving Tradition: Always serve in clay pot (matki) for earthy flavor.
