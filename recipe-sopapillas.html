Mexican

Sopapillas

45 mins Makes 24 Vegetarian
4.6 (274 reviews)

Light, puffy fried pastries dusted with cinnamon sugar and drizzled with honey. These traditional Mexican treats are crisp on the outside, soft and airy inside, and perfect for breakfast, dessert, or as a sweet snack. Our authentic recipe creates perfectly puffed sopapillas every time.

Ingredients

For Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 tbsp shortening or lard
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water

For Frying:

  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

For Coating:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • Honey for drizzling

Instructions

Step 1: Make Dough

Whisk flour, baking powder and salt. Cut in shortening until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add warm water until dough comes together. Knead 5 minutes until smooth. Cover and rest 20 minutes.

Step 2: Roll and Cut

Divide dough into 4 portions. Roll each into 1/8-inch thick rectangle. Cut into 3-inch squares or triangles.

Step 3: Fry Sopapillas

Heat 2 inches oil to 375°F (190°C). Fry 2-3 pieces at a time for 1-2 minutes per side until golden and puffed. Drain on paper towels.

Step 4: Coat and Serve

Mix sugar and cinnamon. While still warm, toss sopapillas in mixture. Drizzle with honey and serve immediately.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Perfect Puff: Ensure oil is at correct temperature - too cool makes them greasy, too hot prevents puffing.

Dough Tip: Resting dough ensures gluten relaxes for maximum puff during frying.

Flavor Variations: Add 1 tsp cinnamon or 1/2 tsp anise seeds to dough for extra flavor.

Make Ahead: Dough can be refrigerated overnight. Bring to room temperature before rolling.
