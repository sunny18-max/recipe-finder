Gulab Jamun

1 hour Serves 6 Medium
4.9 (532 reviews)

Soft, melt-in-mouth milk solids dumplings soaked in rose-scented sugar syrup. This iconic Indian dessert is surprisingly easy to make at home with our foolproof recipe.

Ingredients

For Jamuns:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 3-4 tbsp milk (as needed)

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods
  • 1 tsp rose water
  • Few saffron strands

For Frying:

  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Instructions

Step 1: Make Syrup

Boil sugar and water to 1-string consistency. Add cardamom, rose water and saffron. Keep warm.

Step 2: Prepare Dough

Mix milk powder, flour and baking soda. Add ghee and rub into flour. Gradually add milk to make soft dough.

Step 3: Shape Jamuns

Divide dough into 15-18 equal portions. Roll into smooth balls without cracks.

Step 4: Fry Jamuns

Heat oil on medium-low. Fry jamuns until golden brown, rolling gently for even cooking.

Step 5: Soak in Syrup

Transfer hot jamuns to warm syrup. Let soak for at least 2 hours before serving.

Chef's Notes

Dough Tip: Dough should be soft but not sticky. Over-kneading makes jamuns hard.

Frying Temperature: Maintain medium-low heat (160-170Â°C) for perfect browning.

Storage: Can be refrigerated in syrup for up to 1 week.

Variation: Use khoya/mawa instead of milk powder for richer texture.
