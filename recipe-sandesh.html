Bengali

Sandesh

1 hour Makes 12 pieces Vegetarian
4.7 (356 reviews)

Bengal's iconic milk-based sweet made from fresh chhana (cottage cheese) and flavored with cardamom. These delicate, melt-in-your-mouth confections come in various shapes and flavors, with a texture that's soft yet firm enough to hold its shape, traditionally decorated with pistachios or edible silver leaf.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • ½ tsp cardamom powder

For Garnish:

  • 2 tbsp chopped pistachios
  • Edible silver leaf (optional)
  • Rose water (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Make Chhana

Bring milk to boil. Add lemon juice gradually until milk curdles completely. Strain through muslin cloth and rinse under cold water.

Step 2: Drain and Knead

Hang curdled milk in cloth for 30 mins to drain. Then knead for 10-15 mins until smooth and pliable.

Step 3: Cook Mixture

Transfer to pan, add sugar and cook on low heat, stirring constantly until mixture leaves sides of pan (8-10 mins).

Step 4: Flavor and Shape

Remove from heat, add cardamom. While warm, shape into small discs or use molds. Press pistachios on top.

Step 5: Cool and Serve

Let cool completely before serving. Can be refrigerated for up to 3 days.

Chef's Notes

Milk Quality: Full-fat buffalo milk makes richest sandesh.

Variations: Add mango pulp or saffron for flavored versions.

Texture Tip: Proper kneading ensures smooth texture.

Presentation: Use traditional wooden molds for authentic look.
