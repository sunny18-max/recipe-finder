Rajasthani

Ker Sangri

8 hours (includes soaking) Serves 6 Medium
4.3 (112 reviews)

A traditional Rajasthani delicacy made with ker (desert berries) and sangri (desert beans), sun-dried and cooked with robust spices. This drought-resistant dish has sustained communities for generations with its unique tangy flavor and chewy texture.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried ker (Capparis decidua berries)
  • 1 cup dried sangri (Prosopis cineraria beans)
  • 1/2 cup mustard oil
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 cup yogurt

Spices:

  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp dried mango powder (amchur)
  • 1 tbsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Soak Ingredients

Soak ker and sangri separately overnight (8 hours) in plenty of water. Drain and rinse thoroughly 3-4 times to remove bitterness.

Step 2: Pressure Cook

Cook soaked ker and sangri in pressure cooker with fresh water for 3 whistles (about 20 minutes). Drain completely and set aside.

Step 3: Prepare Base

Heat mustard oil until smoking point. Let cool slightly, then add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and fennel seeds. Sauté until fragrant.

Step 4: Cook Onions

Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 2 minutes until raw smell disappears.

Step 5: Combine Ingredients

Add all dry spices except garam masala. Mix well, then add cooked ker-sangri. Stir to coat with spices.

Step 6: Final Cooking

Add yogurt and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Finish with garam masala and amchur.

Chef's Notes

Mustard Oil: Essential for authentic flavor - don't substitute with other oils.

Texture Tip: Ker should be slightly chewy, not mushy. Don't overcook.

Storage: Tastes better next day. Refrigerates well for up to 1 week.

Serving Tradition: Always served at room temperature with bajra roti or missi roti.

Modern Twist: Add diced potatoes for variation (not traditional but tasty).
