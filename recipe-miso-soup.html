Authentic Miso Soup

15 mins Serves 4 Easy
4.9 (256 reviews)

The ultimate Japanese comfort food - a simple yet deeply flavorful soup made with dashi stock and fermented miso paste. This traditional breakfast staple is packed with umami and can be customized with your favorite ingredients.

Ingredients

For the Dashi:

  • 4 cups water
  • 1 piece kombu (4x4 inch)
  • 1 cup loosely packed bonito flakes

For the Soup:

  • 4 tbsp miso paste (white or red)
  • 1/2 block silken tofu, cubed
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 sheet nori, cut into strips
  • 1 tbsp wakame seaweed (optional)

Optional Add-ins:

  • Mushrooms (shiitake or enoki)
  • Clams or shrimp
  • Daikon radish
  • Egg (poached or soft boiled)

Instructions

Step 1: Make Dashi

Wipe kombu with damp cloth. Soak in cold water 30 minutes, then heat to just below boiling. Remove kombu, bring to boil, add bonito flakes. Turn off heat, steep 5 minutes then strain.

Step 2: Prepare Add-ins

Rehydrate wakame in water if using. Cut tofu, slice green onions, prepare any other ingredients.

Step 3: Dissolve Miso

Ladle 1/2 cup hot dashi into bowl, whisk in miso until smooth. Return to pot (don't boil miso).

Step 4: Assemble

Add tofu and wakame to bowls. Pour hot soup over, then garnish with green onions and nori. Serve immediately.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Miso Tip: White miso is milder, red is stronger. Mix types for balanced flavor.

Shortcut: Use instant dashi powder (1 tsp per cup water) if short on time.

Vegetarian Option: Omit bonito flakes for vegan dashi (kombu-only).

Storage: Keep dashi refrigerated 3 days or frozen 1 month. Don't boil miso soup with ingredients already added.
