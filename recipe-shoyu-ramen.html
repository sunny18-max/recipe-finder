Shoyu Ramen

3 hours Serves 4 Medium
4.9 (412 reviews)

Tokyo-style soy sauce ramen featuring clear brown broth with perfect umami balance. This classic version showcases delicate flavors with springy noodles, marinated eggs, and tender chashu pork slices.

Ingredients

For Broth:

  • 8 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups dashi stock
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 inch ginger, sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed

For Tare (Soy Sauce Base):

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 piece kombu (4x4 inch)
  • 1/2 cup dried bonito flakes

For Toppings:

  • 4 servings fresh ramen noodles
  • 8 slices chashu pork
  • 4 marinated soft-boiled eggs (ajitsuke tamago)
  • 2 green onions, finely sliced
  • 4 sheets nori
  • 1 cup menma (fermented bamboo shoots)
  • Narutomaki fish cake (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Tare

Combine soy sauce, mirin, sake and sugar in saucepan. Add kombu and bonito flakes. Heat until just boiling, then strain.

Step 2: Make Broth

Simmer chicken broth with onion, ginger and garlic for 2 hours. Strain and combine with dashi stock.

Step 3: Marinate Eggs

Soak soft-boiled eggs in 1/4 cup tare mixture for 4-12 hours.

Step 4: Cook Noodles

Boil noodles for 1-2 minutes until al dente. Drain well.

Step 5: Assemble

Add 2-3 tbsp tare to each bowl. Pour hot broth, add noodles, then arrange toppings artfully.

Chef's Notes

Soy Sauce Tip: Use Japanese shoyu (koikuchi) for authentic flavor.

Broth Clarity: Skim impurities regularly for clear broth.

Shortcut: Use quality store-bought dashi packets.

Vegetarian Version: Substitute with shiitake dashi and omit chashu.
