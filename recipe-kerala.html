Kerala Fish Curry

50 mins Serves 4 Non-Vegetarian
4.7 (892 reviews)

The iconic tangy and spicy fish curry from God's Own Country, featuring fresh seafood simmered in a coconut milk gravy with kokum and aromatic spices. This traditional meen curry is cooked in clay pots for authentic flavor and pairs perfectly with steamed rice or appam.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 500g kingfish/seer fish (cut into steaks)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 2 cups thin coconut milk
  • 5-6 kokum petals (or 1 tbsp tamarind pulp)
  • 2 sprigs curry leaves

For Masala Paste:

  • 8-10 dried red chilies
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger, chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 small onion, chopped

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 shallots, sliced

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Masala

Dry roast red chilies, coriander, fenugreek and peppercorns. Grind with turmeric, ginger, garlic and onion to smooth paste.

Step 2: Cook Base

In clay pot or pan, heat oil. Temper mustard seeds, then sauté shallots and curry leaves until golden. Add masala paste and cook for 3 mins.

Step 3: Add Liquids

Pour thin coconut milk and bring to boil. Add kokum/tamarind and salt. Simmer for 10 mins.

Step 4: Cook Fish

Gently add fish pieces. Cook covered on low heat for 8-10 mins until fish is done but not falling apart.

Step 5: Finish

Add thick coconut milk and heat through without boiling. Adjust consistency with water if needed.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Fish Selection: Kingfish, seer fish or pearl spot work best. Avoid delicate fish.

Clay Pot Magic: Cooking in traditional meen chatti enhances flavors.

Kokum Tip: Soak in warm water for 10 mins before using for better extraction.

Leftovers: Tastes even better next day as flavors develop.
