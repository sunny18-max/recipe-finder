Bihari

Litti Chokha

2 hours Serves 4 Vegetarian Wood-fired
4.7 (1,856 reviews)

Bihar's iconic rustic meal - whole wheat balls stuffed with spiced sattu (roasted gram flour) and roasted over cow dung cakes or charcoal, served with smoky mashed vegetable chokha. This wholesome peasant food is packed with protein and earthy flavors, traditionally eaten with hands for the complete experience.

Ingredients

For Litti Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water as needed

For Sattu Filling:

  • 1 cup sattu (roasted gram flour)
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 1 tsp ajwain
  • 1 tsp mango powder (amchur)
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For Baingan Chokha:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Dough

Mix flour, ghee, ajwain and salt. Add water gradually to make stiff dough. Cover and rest for 30 mins.

Step 2: Make Filling

Combine all sattu filling ingredients with enough water to make crumbly mixture that holds shape.

Step 3: Shape Littis

Divide dough into lemon-sized balls. Flatten, add 1 tbsp filling, seal tightly and shape into rounds.

Step 4: Roast

Traditionally roast on cow dung cakes or charcoal. At home, bake at 180Â°C for 25 mins or until golden.

Step 5: Prepare Chokha

Roast eggplant and tomatoes directly on flame. Peel, mash with other ingredients. Season with mustard oil.

Step 6: Serve

Dip hot littis in ghee, serve with chokha, raw onion and green chili. Break open litti to eat.

Chef's Notes

Sattu Tip: Authentic Bihari sattu has distinctive nutty flavor from specific roasting technique.

Smoky Flavor: For authentic taste, char eggplant directly on gas flame until skin blackens.

Alternative Cooking: Can shallow fry littis if baking/roasting not possible.

Serving Style: Traditionally eaten by breaking litti and mixing with chokha.
