Kerala

Meen Molee

40 mins Serves 4 Mild
4.9 (187 reviews)

A delicate coconut milk-based fish stew from Kerala, flavored with ginger, garlic, and curry leaves. This mild yet flavorful dish is traditionally served with appam or bread, showcasing the Syrian Christian culinary heritage of Kerala.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 500g firm white fish (seer fish/pomfret)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1 cup thin coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tbsp ginger, julienned
  • 1 tbsp garlic, sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For Tempering:

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate Fish

Clean and cut fish into steaks. Marinate with turmeric, lemon juice, and salt for 15 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare Base

Heat coconut oil in clay pot. Sauté ginger, garlic, green chilies, and curry leaves until fragrant.

Step 3: Cook Fish

Add thin coconut milk and bring to gentle simmer. Add fish pieces and cook covered for 5-6 minutes.

Step 4: Add Thick Milk

Pour thick coconut milk and heat gently without boiling (2-3 minutes). Remove from heat.

Step 5: Temper

Heat oil in small pan. Splutter mustard seeds, sauté onions until golden. Pour over stew with black pepper.

Chef's Notes

Fish Selection: Seer fish (Neymeen) is traditional, but any firm white fish works.

Coconut Milk: Freshly extracted gives best flavor - first press (thick) and second press (thin).

Traditional Touch: Use mann chatti (clay pot) for authentic earthy flavor.

Serving: Always serve in same clay pot with appam or crusty bread.
