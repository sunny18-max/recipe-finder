Aloo Paratha

1 hour Makes 6 Vegetarian Stuffed
4.9 (4,215 reviews)

The ultimate North Indian comfort food - whole wheat flatbreads stuffed with spiced mashed potato filling. These hearty, flavorful parathas are crispy on the outside with a soft, spicy potato center, perfect for breakfast or any meal. Traditionally served with butter, yogurt, and pickle, they're a complete meal by themselves.

Ingredients

For Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 3/4 cup water (approx)

For Potato Filling:

  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled & mashed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste

For Cooking:

  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • Extra flour for dusting

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Dough

Mix flour, salt and oil. Add water gradually to make soft dough. Knead 5 mins, cover and rest.

Step 2: Make Filling

Mix all filling ingredients. Divide into 6 equal portions.

Step 3: Stuff Parathas

Divide dough into 6 balls. Flatten, place filling, seal edges, and roll gently into 7" circles.

Step 4: Cook Parathas

Heat tawa, cook paratha on medium heat. Flip when bubbles appear, apply ghee, cook both sides.

Step 5: Crisp Up

Press edges gently with spatula to ensure even cooking. Cook until golden brown spots appear.

Step 6: Serve Hot

Apply butter if desired. Serve with yogurt, pickle, or chutney.

Chef's Notes

Masala Tip: Everest or Badshah pav bhaji masala work best. Adjust quantity to taste.

Texture: Bhaji should be thick but spreadable - add water as needed while cooking.

Restaurant Style: Use extra butter and serve on cast iron tawa for authentic experience.

Variations: Add grated beetroot for color or paneer for protein boost.
