Ven Pongal

30 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian
4.6 (215 reviews)

Comforting rice and lentil porridge flavored with cumin, pepper, and ghee. A traditional South Indian breakfast often served during festivals, especially Pongal.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/2 cup split yellow moong dal
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)

For Serving:

  • Coconut chutney
  • Sambar
  • Extra ghee

Instructions

Step 1: Cook Rice and Dal

Wash rice and moong dal together. Pressure cook with 4 cups water for 4-5 whistles until completely soft and mushy.

Step 2: Prepare Tempering

Heat ghee in a pan. Add cumin, peppercorns, ginger, curry leaves, green chilies, and asafoetida. Saut√© until fragrant.

Step 3: Combine

Add the tempering to cooked rice-dal mixture. Mash well with a ladle until creamy. Add salt to taste.

Step 4: Serve

Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, and extra ghee drizzled on top.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Consistency Tip: Authentic pongal should be slightly runny when hot (it thickens as it cools). Add boiling water to adjust.

Pepper Variation: For extra spice, increase peppercorns to 2 tbsp. For milder version, reduce to 2 tsp.

Pressure Cooker Alternative: Can cook in heavy pot (1:4 rice:water ratio) for 30-40 mins, stirring occasionally.

Special Occasion: During Pongal festival, cook outdoors in clay pot with fresh sugarcane juice instead of water.
