Kashmiri

Rogan Josh

3 hours 30 mins Serves 6 Non-Vegetarian
4.8 (723 reviews)

The crown jewel of Kashmiri cuisine - tender lamb braised in a vibrant red gravy infused with aromatic spices like fennel, ginger and Kashmiri chilies. This slow-cooked delicacy gets its signature crimson color from the unique combination of spices and careful cooking technique perfected over generations.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1.5 kg lamb shoulder (bone-in)
  • Â½ cup mustard oil
  • 2 cups yogurt (whisked)
  • 2 tbsp ginger paste
  • 1 tbsp fennel powder
  • 1 tbsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 10-12 green cardamoms
  • 2 black cardamoms
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 4 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste

For Final Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp dried mint leaves
  • 1 tsp rose petals (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate Lamb

Mix lamb with yogurt, ginger paste, fennel powder, chili powder, and salt. Marinate for 2 hours or overnight.

Step 2: Brown Meat

Heat mustard oil until smoking, then cool slightly. Brown marinated lamb in batches on high heat.

Step 3: Slow Cook

Return all meat to pot, add whole spices and 2 cups water. Cover and simmer on low for 2.5 hours until tender.

Step 4: Reduce Gravy

Uncover, increase heat and reduce gravy to desired consistency. Add garam masala.

Step 5: Final Tempering

Heat ghee in small pan, add dried mint and pour over curry. Garnish with rose petals.

Chef's Notes

Mustard Oil Tip: Heating until smoking removes pungency while retaining authentic flavor.

Color Secret: Kashmiri chili provides vibrant color without excessive heat.

Texture Tip: Shoulder cut with bones gives richest flavor and gelatinous texture.

Serving Suggestion: Best enjoyed with steamed rice or Kashmiri naan.
