Assamese

Masor Tenga

40 mins Serves 4 Non-Vegetarian Sour Curry
4.8 (967 reviews)

The signature sour fish curry from Assam, featuring freshwater fish cooked in a light broth with outenga (elephant apple) or lemon for tartness. This delicate yet flavorful dish is a staple in Assamese households, known for its refreshing sour notes and minimal use of spices that let the fish's natural flavor shine through.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 500g Rohu or Catla fish (cut into steaks)
  • 1 outenga (elephant apple) or 2 lemons
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil

For Flavoring:

  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds (ground to paste)
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish:

  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 green chili, sliced

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Souring Agent

If using outenga, cut into wedges and remove seeds. If using lemon, cut into halves.

Step 2: Marinate Fish

Rub fish pieces with 1/2 tsp turmeric and salt. Keep aside for 10 minutes.

Step 3: Temper Oil

Heat mustard oil until smoking point. Add fenugreek seeds and let splutter.

Step 4: Cook Base

Add tomatoes and green chilies. Saut√© until tomatoes turn mushy.

Step 5: Prepare Curry

Add water, remaining turmeric, and outenga/lemon. Bring to boil.

Step 6: Cook Fish

Gently add fish pieces. Simmer for 8-10 minutes until fish is cooked.

Step 7: Finish

Add mustard paste and coriander leaves. Remove from heat immediately.

Chef's Notes

Fish Selection: Freshwater fish like Rohu or Catla work best for authentic flavor.

Souring Options: Outenga provides subtle tartness while lemon gives sharper tang.

Mustard Paste: Authentic version uses ground mustard seeds for pungency.

Serving Suggestion: Best enjoyed with steamed rice and aloo pitika (mashed potatoes).
