Odia

Pakhala Bhata

8 hours (fermentation) Serves 4 Vegetarian Cooling Dish
4.9 (2,043 reviews)

Odisha's traditional fermented rice dish, perfect for scorching summers. Leftover rice is fermented overnight in water, creating a probiotic-rich, cooling meal typically served with fried fish, roasted vegetables, and an array of accompaniments. This ancient preparation method enhances digestibility while providing natural cooling properties.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably leftover)
  • 4 cups water (at room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (optional for faster fermentation)
  • 1 tsp salt

Traditional Accompaniments:

  • 1 fried fish (Bhetki or Rohu)
  • 1 cup roasted vegetables (eggplant, potato, pumpkin)
  • 1/2 cup badi chura (crushed lentil dumplings)
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves

For Tempering (Optional):

  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 tsp panch phoron
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Rice

Take leftover rice in earthen pot or glass container. Break up any lumps gently with fingers.

Step 2: Add Water

Pour room temperature water until rice is fully submerged (about 1 inch above rice level). Add salt.

Step 3: Ferment

Cover with muslin cloth and let ferment at room temperature for 6-8 hours (overnight is traditional). For faster fermentation, mix in yogurt.

Step 4: Prepare Tempering

Heat mustard oil until smoking. Add panch phoron, red chilies and garlic. Pour over fermented rice water mixture.

Chef's Notes

Earthen Pot Magic: Fermenting in matka (clay pot) enhances flavor and probiotic content.

Summer Essential: The sourness indicates proper fermentation - more sour means more cooling effect.

Digestive Aid: The fermentation creates beneficial bacteria that aid digestion.

Regional Variations: In coastal areas, sea water was traditionally used instead of salt.
