Bengali

Machher Jhol

40 mins Serves 4 Medium
4.6 (342 reviews)

A light and flavorful Bengali fish curry made with mustard oil and aromatic spices. This comforting dish features tender fish pieces in a thin, turmeric-tinged gravy that's perfect with steamed rice.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 500g rohu or katla fish (steaks)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp mustard oil

For Gravy:

  • 1 large potato, cut into wedges
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala

For Tempering:

  • 1 tsp kalonji (nigella seeds)
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 bay leaf

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate Fish

Rub fish with 1/2 tsp turmeric and 1/2 tsp salt. Keep aside for 10 minutes.

Step 2: Fry Fish

Heat 2 tbsp mustard oil until smoking. Fry fish 2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.

Step 3: Prepare Gravy

In same oil, add kalonji, bay leaf and chilies. Add potatoes and fry until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and all spices.

Step 4: Cook Curry

Add tomatoes and cook until mushy. Add 2 cups water and simmer until potatoes are tender.

Step 5: Add Fish

Gently add fried fish. Simmer for 5 minutes. Finish with garam masala and remaining mustard oil.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Fish Selection: Use freshwater fish like rohu, katla or hilsa for authentic flavor.

Mustard Oil: Essential for authentic taste. Heat until smoking to remove pungency.

Variation: Add eggplant or pumpkin for different textures.

Serving Suggestion: Best enjoyed with steamed rice and lemon wedges.
