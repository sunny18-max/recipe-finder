Rajasthani

Dal Baati Churma

2.5 hours Serves 6 Vegetarian
4.7 (389 reviews)

Rajasthan's iconic trio of flavors - crispy whole wheat baatis (dough balls) served with a mix of five lentil dal and sweet crumbled churma. This hearty meal combines textures and tastes perfectly, with the baatis' crusty exterior giving way to a soft interior that soaks up the spicy dal, balanced by the sweet churma.

Ingredients

For Baati:

  • 3 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1 cup ghee
  • 1 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Water as needed

For Panchmel Dal:

  • ¼ cup each: toor dal, chana dal, urad dal, moong dal, masoor dal
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies

For Churma:

  • 6 cooked baatis
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • ½ cup ghee
  • 1 tsp cardamom powder

Instructions

Step 1: Make Baati Dough

Mix flour, semolina, ¼ cup ghee, ajwain and salt. Add water to make stiff dough. Rest for 30 mins.

Step 2: Shape and Bake

Make lemon-sized balls, flatten slightly. Bake at 180°C for 30 mins until golden, brushing with ghee halfway.

Step 3: Cook Dal

Pressure cook washed dals with turmeric and salt (3 whistles). Temper with ghee, cumin, garlic, ginger, and spices.

Step 4: Prepare Churma

Crumble baked baatis, fry in ghee until crisp. Powder with sugar and cardamom when cooled.

Step 5: Serve

Break hot baatis, pour ghee over. Serve with dal and churma on the side.

Chef's Notes

Traditional Method: Baatis were originally cooked in desert sand!

Ghee Tip: Use homemade ghee for authentic flavor.

Serving Suggestion: Accompany with garlic chutney and onion salad.

Shortcut: Use pre-mixed panchmel dal if available.
