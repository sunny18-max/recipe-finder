Mexican Flan

1 hr 15 mins 4 hrs chilling Serves 8
4.9 (412 reviews)

Silky smooth caramel custard with a rich golden caramel sauce, this classic Mexican dessert is the perfect balance of sweet and creamy. Our authentic recipe uses simple ingredients to create a luxurious texture that melts in your mouth, making it an impressive yet surprisingly easy dessert for any occasion.

Ingredients

For Caramel:

  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water

For Custard:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 (14 oz) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 (12 oz) can evaporated milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Caramel

Combine sugar and water in saucepan over medium heat. Cook without stirring until amber-colored (about 10 minutes). Immediately pour into baking dish, swirling to coat bottom evenly. Set aside to harden.

Step 2: Make Custard

In blender, combine eggs, milks, vanilla and salt. Blend until smooth (about 30 seconds). Strain through fine mesh sieve into prepared pan with caramel.

Step 3: Bake in Water Bath

Place filled pan in larger roasting pan. Pour hot water into roasting pan until halfway up sides of flan dish. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 50-60 minutes until set but still jiggly in center.

Step 4: Chill Thoroughly

Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate at least 4 hours (preferably overnight) to fully set.

Step 5: Unmold and Serve

Run knife around edges. Place serving plate upside down over pan and quickly invert. Let caramel sauce flow over flan before removing pan. Serve chilled.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Caramel Tip: Watch carefully as sugar can burn quickly once it starts changing color.

Smooth Texture: Straining the custard ensures perfectly smooth flan without bubbles.

Flavor Variations: Add 1 tsp cinnamon or 2 tbsp coffee liqueur to custard for different flavors.

Make Ahead: Flan keeps well refrigerated for up to 3 days - flavors improve over time.
