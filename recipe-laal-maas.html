Rajasthani

Laal Maas

2 hours 30 mins Serves 4-6 Spicy
4.7 (243 reviews)

The fiery signature dish of Rajasthan, Laal Maas is a bold mutton curry where tender meat is cooked in a sauce of Mathania red chilies, garlic, and yogurt. Originally a hunter's dish, it gets its distinctive red color from the special chilies of the region.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 kg mutton (goat meat), bone-in
  • 15-20 dried Mathania red chilies (or Kashmiri chilies)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • 10-12 garlic cloves
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 1/2 cup mustard oil

Whole Spices:

  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2 inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds

Powdered Spices:

  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Chili Paste

Soak red chilies in hot water for 30 minutes. Blend into smooth paste with garlic and ginger.

Step 2: Marinate Mutton

Mix mutton with half the chili paste, yogurt, and salt. Marinate for at least 2 hours (overnight preferred).

Step 3: Cook Onions

Heat mustard oil until smoking point. Reduce heat, add whole spices and sliced onions. Cook until golden brown.

Step 4: Cook Mutton

Add marinated mutton and remaining chili paste. Cook on high heat for 5 minutes, then reduce to low.

Step 5: Slow Cook

Add 2 cups water, cover and simmer for 1.5-2 hours until meat is tender. Finish with garam masala.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Chili Note: Authentic Mathania chilies are mild but give vibrant color. Adjust quantity if using hotter varieties.

Oil Importance: Mustard oil is traditional and adds distinct flavor - don't substitute.

Meat Choice: Use shoulder or leg cuts with some fat for best results.

Serving: Traditionally served with bajra roti or steamed rice with ghee.
