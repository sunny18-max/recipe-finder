Bangladeshi

Shorshe Ilish

45 mins Serves 4 Medium
4.9 (412 reviews)

Hilsa fish cooked in a pungent mustard sauce, this iconic Bengali dish showcases the prized ilish fish in a bold, flavorful gravy. The combination of freshly ground mustard paste with green chilies and nigella seeds creates a complex flavor profile that perfectly complements the rich, oily fish.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 4 pieces hilsa (ilish) fish
  • 1/2 cup mustard seeds (yellow/black mix)
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder

For Tempering:

  • 3 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds

For Gravy:

  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Mustard Paste

Soak mustard seeds in warm water for 15 minutes. Drain and grind with green chilies and little water to smooth paste.

Step 2: Marinate Fish

Rub fish pieces with turmeric and salt. Let sit 10 minutes.

Step 3: Make Tempering

Heat mustard oil until smoking. Cool slightly, then add nigella and fenugreek seeds.

Step 4: Cook Gravy

Add mustard paste, turmeric, chili powder to oil. Cook 2 minutes. Add water, sugar and salt. Bring to simmer.

Step 5: Cook Fish

Gently add fish pieces. Cover and simmer 10-12 minutes until fish is cooked through.

Video Tutorial

Chef's Notes

Mustard Tip: Use combination of yellow and black mustard seeds for authentic flavor.

Fish Alternative: Salmon or mackerel can substitute for hilsa if unavailable.

Tempering Trick: Mustard oil must smoke then cool slightly to remove raw taste.

Serving Suggestion: Best enjoyed with steamed rice to balance the strong flavors.
