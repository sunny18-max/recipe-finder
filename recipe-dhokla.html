Gujarati

Dhokla

30 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian
4.7 (342 reviews)

Fluffy, savory steamed cakes made from fermented chickpea flour, tempered with mustard seeds and curry leaves. This iconic Gujarati snack is light, protein-rich, and served with sweet-tangy chutneys.

Ingredients

For Batter:

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • 1 tsp ginger-green chili paste
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp fruit salt (eno)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3/4 cup water

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Batter

Mix besan, semolina, ginger-chili paste, lemon juice, sugar, turmeric, salt and water to smooth batter. Rest for 15 minutes.

Step 2: Steam

Grease steaming tray. Add fruit salt to batter, mix gently and pour immediately. Steam for 15-20 minutes.

Step 3: Temper

Heat oil, add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, curry leaves and chilies. Pour over steamed dhokla.

Step 4: Garnish

Mix sugar in water and sprinkle over dhokla. Garnish with cilantro and coconut.

Step 5: Serve

Cut into squares and serve with green chutney and tamarind chutney.

Chef's Notes

Batter Tip: For softer dhokla, use 1:1 ratio of besan to yogurt instead of water.

Steaming: Keep water boiling before adding batter. Don't open lid while steaming.

Variations: Khaman dhokla uses split chickpeas (chana dal) instead of besan.

Serving: Best served warm - becomes dense when refrigerated.
