Rajma Chawal

1 hour 30 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian
4.7 (892 reviews)

A classic North Indian comfort food featuring red kidney beans cooked in a rich, aromatic tomato-based gravy served with steamed basmati rice. This hearty and nutritious dish is a staple in Punjabi households, known for its creamy texture and perfect balance of spices.

Ingredients

For Rajma:

  • 1 cup dried red kidney beans (or 2 cans)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

For Rice:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Rajma

Soak kidney beans overnight or for 8 hours. Pressure cook with 3 cups water and salt for 15-20 minutes until tender. If using canned beans, rinse well.

Step 2: Make Gravy

Heat ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds, then onions. Sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell disappears. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.

Step 3: Add Spices

Add all dry spices (turmeric, coriander, cumin, red chili powder) and cook for 2 minutes. Add cooked rajma with its water and simmer for 15-20 minutes.

Step 4: Prepare Rice

Wash and soak rice for 20 minutes. Heat ghee, add whole spices, then drained rice. Add water and salt. Cook covered until done (about 15 minutes).

Step 5: Final Touches

Mash some rajma to thicken gravy. Add garam masala and kasuri methi. Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Chef's Notes

Texture Tip: For creamier rajma, mash some beans against the pan while cooking.

Time Saver: Use canned kidney beans when short on time (reduce cooking time to 10 mins).

Flavor Boost: Add 1 tbsp butter at the end for restaurant-style richness.

Variation: Try adding 1 tsp anardana powder (pomegranate seed powder) for tanginess.
