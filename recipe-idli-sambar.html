Idli Sambar

40 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian
4.7 (287 reviews)

Soft, fluffy steamed rice cakes served with aromatic lentil stew and coconut chutney. A healthy and comforting breakfast that's perfect for starting your day with authentic South Indian flavors!

Ingredients

For Idli:

  • 2 cups idli rice
  • 1 cup urad dal
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

For Sambar:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (drumsticks, carrot, pumpkin)
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • Few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

For Coconut Chutney:

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1/2 inch ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • Few curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Idli Batter

Soak rice and urad dal separately for 4-6 hours with fenugreek seeds. Grind urad dal to a smooth fluffy batter and rice to a slightly coarse consistency. Mix together with salt and ferment overnight (8-12 hours) in a warm place until the batter doubles in volume.

Step 2: Make Sambar

Pressure cook toor dal with turmeric until completely soft. In a separate pot, cook the vegetables until tender. Mix the cooked dal with vegetables, sambar powder, tamarind water, and salt. Bring to a boil. Temper with mustard seeds, dried red chilies, and curry leaves in hot oil.

Step 3: Prepare Chutney

Grind coconut with green chilies, ginger, salt and enough water to make a smooth paste. Heat oil in a small pan, add mustard seeds and curry leaves. When they splutter, pour this tempering over the chutney and mix well.

Step 4: Steam Idlis

Grease idli molds lightly with oil. Pour the fermented batter into each mold, filling about 3/4 full. Steam in a pressure cooker (without whistle) or idli steamer for 10-12 minutes until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Let rest for 2 minutes before removing from molds.

Step 5: Serve

Serve hot idlis with warm sambar and coconut chutney. For authentic experience, place 2-3 idlis in a bowl, pour sambar over them, and enjoy with chutney on the side.

Chef's Notes

Fermentation Tip: Keep batter in oven with light on for consistent warmth. In cold climates, extend fermentation time up to 24 hours.

Batter Consistency: The ideal batter should be thick but pourable, similar to pancake batter.

Sambar Variations: Add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) to the tempering for authentic flavor. You can also include pearl onions for extra sweetness.

Storage: Leftover batter can be refrigerated for 2-3 days. Bring to room temperature before use.

Quick Version: For instant idlis, use ready-made idli rava or semolina (sooji) mixed with yogurt.
